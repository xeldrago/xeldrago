What is Phishing and How to Avoid It

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where a hacker masquerades as a trusted entity, usually via email or fake websites, to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card details, or personal data.

Types of Phishing:

  • Link Manipulation: Hackers send fake links that look real, but when clicked, they lead to malicious sites.
  • Fake Websites: Attackers create duplicate websites resembling real services (like Instagram or Facebook) to collect login credentials.
  • Malware Distribution: Hackers trick users into downloading malware through deceptive emails or pop-up ads.

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Always verify URLs before entering sensitive information.
  • Check for HTTPS encryption in the browser address bar.
  • Don't click on suspicious links or download files from untrusted sources.
